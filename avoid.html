Areas to Avoid




The post-World War II environment still contained heavy amounts of anti-Semitic sentiments, which often manifested as forms of violence. This violence often came about as pogroms; violent riots in which Jews were attacked. These occurred not only in Eastern Europe, but also in the USA and under other Allied mandates. ALthough riots themselves were normally one-time occurrences, sentiments harbored by people who participated in such riots remained even after they were over. Below is compiled a non-exhaustive list of areas known to be unwelcome to Jewish displaced persons. Citizens at the time would potentially have been able to add areas to the list as based on personal observations, the news, or from other verified sources.
